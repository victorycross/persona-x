**For sole proprietors offering structured AI challenge to small businesses and fellow sole traders**

Version 1.0 | 13 February 2026

---

## The Opportunity

Sole proprietors and small business owners make high-stakes decisions without
the benefit of a board, an advisory committee, or specialist functions. They
rely on their own judgement, a trusted accountant, and maybe a mentor. When
they get it wrong, there is no safety net.

Persona-x turns this gap into a service opportunity. A sole proprietor with
access to Persona-x can offer **structured multi-perspective challenge as a
web-based service** — giving small business clients access to the kind of
deliberate, governed reasoning that larger organisations build entire teams
to provide.

Each service below can be delivered as a standalone web product with a clear
value proposition, a defined panel of personas, and a repeatable delivery
model.

---

## How Each Service Works

Every service follows the same pattern:

1. **Client submits** their document, decision, or scenario through a web form
2. **Persona-x assembles** a pre-built panel of personas designed for that
   service, each with calibrated rubric scores, defined boundaries, and
   specific failure modes to surface
3. **The panel challenges** the submission from multiple defined perspectives
4. **Client receives** a structured report showing what each persona flagged,
   where perspectives diverged, and what questions remain unresolved
5. **The report is auditable** — the client can see which reasoning profile
   produced which challenge, and the personas are versioned artefacts

## Service Packaging

### Tiered Model

| Tier | What the Client Gets | Price Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Quick Challenge** | Single panel run, summary report | $49-149 |
| **Deep Review** | Panel run + follow-up questions + revised challenge | $199-399 |
| **Advisory Package** | Quarterly panel reviews + persona library | $999-2,499/year |

### How to Build This Business

1. **Start with three services** that share personas. Business Plan Stress
   Test, Pricing Strategy Challenge, and Proposal Review use overlapping
   persona types (financial realist, customer advocate, risk surfacer). Build
   these personas once and reuse them across services.

2. **Productise the delivery**. Each service has a fixed web form, a fixed
   panel, and a structured report template. The client submits, the panel
   runs, the report generates. Your time is in quality control and persona
   refinement, not in delivering each review manually.

3. **Build industry-specific persona libraries**. A "Financial Realist"
   persona for a trades business has different rubric calibration than one
   for a professional services firm. Industry specialisation increases
   perceived value and allows premium pricing.

4. **Use the audit trail as a differentiator**. Every report shows which
   persona raised which concern, with what rubric profile. This
   transparency is the product — the client is not buying a black-box
   opinion, they are buying structured, inspectable challenge.

5. **Scale through partnerships**. Accountants, bookkeepers, business
   coaches, and brokers all have small business clients who need these
   services. White-label the panel review under a partner's brand, or
   offer it as a value-add to their existing advisory relationship.
